# LinkedIn & GitHub Profile Optimization

Your online presence is your 24/7 portfolio. While you sleep, recruiters are searching for candidates on LinkedIn. While you're at your current job, hiring managers are evaluating your GitHub. Let's make sure both profiles are working hard for you.

## Why This Matters

Here's the reality:
- 87% of recruiters use LinkedIn to find candidates
- 70% of employers check GitHub profiles before interviews
- A strong online presence can lead to opportunities finding YOU instead of the other way around
- Your profiles are often reviewed BEFORE your resume

Think of LinkedIn as your professional brand and GitHub as your technical portfolio. Together, they tell your complete story.

## Part 1: LinkedIn Optimization

### The LinkedIn Algorithm: How Recruiters Find You

Recruiters search LinkedIn using keywords. If your profile doesn't contain the right terms, you won't appear in their searches. It's that simple.

**How LinkedIn Ranking Works:**
1. Keyword matching (your profile vs. search terms)
2. Profile completeness (100% > 90%)
3. Engagement (posts, comments, shares)
4. Connection strength (1st degree connections rank higher)
5. Recency (recently updated profiles rank higher)

Your goal: Optimize for all five factors.

### Profile Photo: First Impressions Matter

Your photo is the first thing people see. Make it count.

**Good Profile Photo:**
- Professional but approachable
- Clear, well-lit face shot
- Solid or simple background
- Current (within 2 years)
- You're smiling and looking at camera
- Business casual or professional attire
- High resolution

**Bad Profile Photo:**
- Group photo (which one are you?)
- Too casual (beach photos, party pics)
- Dark or blurry
- Wearing sunglasses
- Cropped from another photo
- Too far away (can't see your face)
- No photo at all (massive missed opportunity)

**Pro Tip:** Profiles with photos get 21x more views and 9x more connection requests.

Don't have a professional photo? Use your phone:
1. Find a plain wall or simple background
2. Natural lighting (near a window)
3. Dress professionally
4. Ask a friend to take 20-30 shots
5. Pick the most natural, friendly one

### Skills Section: Get Found in Searches

You can add up to 50 skills. The top 3 appear on your profile. Choose wisely.

**How to Order Your Skills:**

**Top 3 (Most Visible):**
1. Your primary language (JavaScript, Python, etc.)
2. Your main framework (React, Django, etc.)
3. Your strongest technical skill (Node.js, AWS, etc.)

**Next 10-15 (Important for searches):**
- Specific technologies you use regularly
- Tools and platforms you're proficient in
- Relevant methodologies (Agile, CI/CD)

**Remaining (Good to have):**
- Technologies you've used but aren't primary
- Soft skills (Communication, Leadership)
- Languages you speak

**Skills Strategy:**
- Prioritize skills mentioned in job descriptions
- Get endorsements from colleagues
- Add new skills as you learn them
- Remove outdated skills
- Rearrange based on what you want to emphasize

**Types:**

- `docs:` Documentation changes
- `test:` Test additions/changes
- `chore:` Maintenance tasks
